Finance Reports

Financial reporting is crucial not only for tracking finances but also for understanding significant changes within your organization. Tithing is an important indicator of your organization's health. By analyzing giving patterns, you can identify direct correlations between people and their contributions. For example, you can reach out to individuals who have recently stopped giving to see if they need support or prayer, and you can also connect with those who have recently started giving to learn about the positive changes happening in their lives.

Rock introduced two new finance reporting features in version 17. Your finance team can now view or generate:

- Volunteer Generosity Report
- Tithing Overview
- Transaction Fee Report

For some reports, the goal is not to see total dollar amounts but to be able to check in on your congregation and follow the data to better serve your organization.

Below are details explaining these new reports in Rock. Please note, for some blocks, data is based on estimations. This is not the area that will give exact dollar amounts (that can be found on a person profile page). The goal is to show key areas that need attention to increase potential tithe opportunities. To view these reports, go to Finance \> Reports.

# Volunteer Generosity

This report is used to compare and capture insights on your volunteer team and their giving. It shows which volunteers have donated within a specific time range and lets you filter by Attendance Date Ranges, Campuses, and Teams. This helps you understand how volunteering relates to financial giving.

# Just a Note

This tool tracks the connection between volunteers' giving and their service, not their exact donation amounts.

# How Rock Defines a Volunteer

A volunteer is any active person who has recorded attendance at least once within the past year in a group where the group type's *Purpose* is set to *"Serving Area"*. This can be set at Admin Tools \> Settings \> Group Types in the group type's *General* settings.

# Tithing Overview Report

The Tithing Overview Report allows Rock Finance Teams (or your organization's leadership team) to see how their campuses are performing with overall estimated tithing. The estimations on this report could be used to foster discussions on how and when to encourage giving within your organization.

**Tithing Households (%)** represents the percentage of giving households estimated to be tithing. A "giving household" is defined as one that has contributed to a tax-deductible account within the past 365 days.

This report leverages US Census, location information, and average income figures to estimate potential tithe amounts, referred to as the "Tithe Index". For example, if the average household income for all 'givers' at a particular campus is $100,000, the Tithe Index would be $10,000, suggesting each person at that campus might tithe approximately $192 per week. Since average household income varies by postal code, the Tithe Index will differ across campuses, reflecting local economic conditions.

Rock comes populated with a schedule that runs once a week to calculate tithing metric data (you can adapt this schedule to meet your organization's needs if necessary). If needed, you can access this by going to Admin Tools \> Settings \> Schedules \> Tithing Overview. The calculated data is stored into three separate metrics under Tools \> Metrics \> Tithing Overview.

There are two views within this report, the Bar Graph View and Line Graph View.

- **Bar Graph View**: Displays the most recent set of data with each bar color coded based on the age (Opened Date) of the campus.
- **Line Graph View**: Will display a rolling 12 month set of data for each campus, with each line color coded based on the Color of the campus.

# Opened Date and Color

The campus Opened Date and Color can be set by going to Admin Tools \> Settings \> Campuses.

# Transaction Fee Report

The Transaction Fee Report let's the finance team track account gifts and the "covered" fees associated with the gift. The Transaction Fee Report breaks down the Total Fees, Credit Card Fees and ACH Fees, showing total transactions and the fees associated with each transaction. You can set specific time frames to see what each giving period brings. This report will help gain insight into all the fees associated with gift transactions.
