Interactive Experiences

Interactive Experiences are your key to creating engaging and dynamic church services. With this feature, you can foster real-time connections through interactive questions in your church’s Rock Mobile app. You can choose between anonymous or personalized responses, enable your pastor to connect with the congregation in real time, and add a new level of engagement to your services. Let’s look at the general process before we get into specifics.

![Interactive Experience Devices](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.17.0/images/interactive-experiences-devices-16.png)

Interactive Experience Devices

Pictured below is an example of a poll question. That’s one type of *Action* that your *Experience* can have. Another type of Action is asking a short answer question.

![Action on Device](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.17.0/images/interactive-experiences-action-on-device-v16.png)

Action on Device

Aside from Actions, there are other customizable screens that the person can see. For before the service you can create a custom Welcome screen with your own image and colors.

![Welcome Screen](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.17.0/images/interactive-experiences-welcome-v16.png)

Welcome Screen

But what about in the middle of the service, between Actions? This feature provides a customizable screen for that too. And then at the end of the experience, you guessed it, you can customize what people will see.

# Rock Mobile Only

See the [Rock Mobile documentation](https://mobiledocs.rockrms.com/) for information on configuring your app for use with this feature.

Before we dive into the setup, let’s look at a few other features that come along with Interactive Experiences.

# Experience Manager

You can access the different Interactive Experience pages in Rock by going to Tools \> Interactive Experiences. First, we’ll look at the Experience Manager.

![Experience Manager - Live Event](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-experience-manager-live-event-v18.png)

Experience Manager - Live Event

The Experience Manager is typically what you’ll use to, that’s right, manage the experiences. Pictured above, under the Live Event tab, you can see a live count of how many people are participating, and you get to control, in real time, the Actions that will show up on people’s mobile devices. It even includes a preview window so you can see what they see.

Under the Moderation tab you can review responses to questions, particularly short answer questions, and can approve or reject them based on their content.

![Experience Manager - Moderation](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-experience-manager-moderation-v18.png)

Experience Manager - Moderation

The last stop in our tour of the Experience Manager is the Live Questions tab. This is where you can see a list of responses to the selection action. This list is filterable by campus. Only responses that have been approved (for those requiring approval) will appear here.

![Experience Manager - Live Questions](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-experience-manager-live-questions-v18.png)

Experience Manager - Live Questions

# Experience Questions

This area is meant for the pastor on stage, to view the results of the actions (e.g., poll answers) live and in real time as people are responding through your church’s app.

![Experience Questions](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-experience-questions-v18.png)

Experience Questions

As pictured above, the pastor can select which question to view. And there’s an optional Campus filter, to ensure they’re viewing the correct set of replies.

# Experience Visualizer

The answers to your questions can be read in a list like we’ve seen so far. But the Experience Visualizer lets you see the responses graphically.

![Visualizer Graph](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.17.0/images/interactive-experiences-visualizer-graph-v16.png)

Visualizer Graph

You can also see the responses presented as a word cloud. The larger the word, the more responses it got.

![Visualizer Word Cloud](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.17.0/images/interactive-experiences-visualizer-word-cloud-v16.png)

Visualizer Word Cloud

# Experience Administration

Experience Administration is where you’ll go to create and maintain your experiences, actions and what gets displayed on the app.

Let’s walk through setting up a new experience. We’ll start by adding a new row to the Experience Administration page under Tools \> Interactive Experiences.

![Add New Experience](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-add-new-exp-1-v18.png)

Add New Experience

Next, we’ll look at the schedule configuration. As it says on the page, Schedules determine when and where the experience occurs.

![Add New Experience](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-add-new-exp-2-v18.png)

Add New Experience

As we move down the page, you'll find additional settings for what people will see in your app during the experience.

![Add New Experience](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-add-new-exp-3-v18.png)

Add New Experience

Below is an example of what the No Actions Content might look like.

![Stay Tuned](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.17.0/images/interactive-experiences-stay-tuned-v16.png)

Stay Tuned

As we continue down the configuration list, we get to customize what the person sees when the experience has ended. You'll also see options to provide a custom theme for the experience.

![Add New Experience](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-add-new-exp-4-v18.png)

Add New Experience

Next, let’s dive into the action configuration, which you would access from the same page, as pictured in the last screenshot below. What you’ll see will vary depending on the Type of action you’re working with. We’ll use an example from the Poll action type.

![Add New Experience](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-add-action-v18.png)

Add New Experience

After you’ve got your actions configured, you’re ready to go live with this experience! With everything set up, your Experience Administration page will look similar to the below screenshot.

![Experience Administration](https://rockrms.blob.core.windows.net/documentation/Books/14/1.18.0/images/interactive-experiences-experience-administration-v18.png)

Experience Administration

We can edit the configuration for this experience by clicking the Edit button pictured above. Below that you can edit the Actions or add new ones.
